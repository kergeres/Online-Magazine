    Snakes for beginners

    There are over 3000 type of snakes in the world, so it's not easy to find out which one is perfect for you. Counting in habitat, expenses and time time requirements we I collected the most recommended top 5 snake for beginner.

    1. Corn Snakes

    Corn snakes can be found in a wide range of sizes. They are not 'teeny' snakes, but they are a good size for beginners who would like a snake big enough to hold in both hands, but not frighteningly big.

    There are some larger species, but they have thin bodies that make them look shorter than their actual lenght. Corns snakes are a great option, and recommended often for beginners, because they are easy to care for, and come in various colours. Just be sure to get a captive-bred animal.

    2. Children’s Python

    This species is certainly for you, if you’re looking for a 'mini python’. The Children's python (named after a person, not their size or suitability for children) is easy to breed and keep, and they reach a lenght no more than one meter, which is very small for a snake.

    These miniature animals can be kept in a 60 liter tank, but even two snakes will be fine in a 75 liter. This species feeds mostly on frogs and lizards in the wild, so naturally they can be finicky when younger, which is the case with most snakes. Available Children's pythons are usually acclimated to eating frozen/thawed mice.

    There are a few other species of python that are very similar in appearance to the Children's python and are closely related. The smallest python in the world, the Anthill python reaches only 24-30 inches in length. There is also the large-blotched python and the spotted python.These snakes need similar care.

    Size

    70 - 100 cm

    Temperament

    Calm as adults.
    A little more feisty as babies.

    Feeding Response

    Adults feed on frozen/thawed mice.

    3. African Egg-Eating Snake

    While these unusual snakes are not readily available like the others on this list, they are surprisingly easy to care for and are the only species that can be considered to be a ‘vegetarian’.

    African egg-eating snakes, as their name suggests, feed exclusively on eggs and require no whole animal prey. The only catch is that you will need a reliable source of extra small quail or finch eggs that are preferably fertilized. These eggs can be found in Asian food markets, online, and sometimes bird breeders will provide them, however some of these are not fertilized.

    Many people sells small eggs that are fertilized, although some have fully developed chicks that cannot be fed. When using frozen eggs, defrost in warm water and lightly shake until you detect ‘sloshing’ in the inside, then feed immediately. If you can locate their food, these small snakes make lovely pets that have no front teeth but rarely bite anyway.

    Size

    60 - 90 cm

    Temperament

    Very easy to handle, really recommended

    Feeding Response

    Slow feeding response

    4. Western Hognose

These snakes are greyish, light oil green coloured, with approximately 40 darker green spots on their backs. Their stomach is mostly white, but at the tail they are black. Their head is lighter than the body, and it has dark brown streaks on the top and behind the eyes. These snakes have 23 rows of scaling on the back. The most prominent characteristic of these snakes might be their upright nose that they use for digging.

    5. Rainbow Boa

    The rainbow boa is a land snake from the family of the Boaide. Most of it’s species has the same back pattern, darker rings on it’s orange-brown skin. Excluded are the panaman and colombian snakes, these are plain brown. The rings of the body are called eye patches, and they are the most noticable on younger snakes, because they have the most vibrant colour in all of the species. The scales of rainbow boa look smooth, but have microscopic grooves, that split the light to it’s colors, when the animal is moving. That is where the name rainbow boa originates from. The most colorful species is the brazilian rainbow boa

    Size

    50 - 70 cm

    Temperament

    Some individuals can be flighty.
